If you liked the idea of a Holi celebration at Marymoor, here's another fun, novel
cultural day — and it's close. The **Skandia Midsommarfest** brings a Swedish midsummer
celebration to **Saint Edward State Park** in Kenmore on **Sunday, June 28, 11am–6pm**.
The centerpiece is the **traditional pole raising and parade at 2:30pm**, followed by the
*långdans* — a serpentine folk dance that snakes around the flower-decorated midsommarstång
— with anyone welcome to join in. Two stages run live Nordic music and dance through the
day, plus flower-crown making, a children's corner, and a Scandinavian crafts market
(Hemslöjdsmarknad).

There's food to match: Scandinavian offerings, coffee and ice cream, and **Finn the Food
Truck** from Anthony's. It's an easy, festive afternoon and a genuinely good one for kids.

Best of all, it's barely a trip: Saint Edward State Park sits on the north end of Lake
Washington, about a **15–20 minute drive from downtown Redmond** up Juanita Drive — no
city, no ferry, no fuss. **Admission is free**, but it's a Washington state park, so
you'll need a **[Discover Pass](https://www.discoverpass.wa.gov/)** ($10 day / $30 annual)
to park; the lots fill on event days, so go early or be ready to walk in a bit. The park
itself is worth the visit on its own — big forest, trails down to the lake, and the grand
old seminary building — so it's an easy one to make an afternoon of.

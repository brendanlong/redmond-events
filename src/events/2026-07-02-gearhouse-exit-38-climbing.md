Gearhouse is running **weekly Thursday evening rock climbing sessions at Exit 38** on I-90 through August — outdoor sport climbing on actual stone, with a crew of members ranging from beginners to people sending pumpy overhangs. It's a carpool event that leaves from Capitol Hill, and the access from Redmond is actually pretty clean: jump on I-90 heading east, and you're there in about 35–40 minutes without crossing any bridges.

Exit 38 (near North Bend) is one of the closest accessible outdoor climbing spots to the Seattle metro area — bolted sport routes at all grades, roadside access, and good sun in the afternoon. For anyone who's been bouldering indoors and wants to try real rock this summer, this is the natural next step.

The sessions run **4:30–9 p.m. every Thursday**, July 2 through August 6. Gearhouse arranges carpooling from their Capitol Hill shop, which is the intended assembly point — if you're coming from Redmond, you can either pick up riders in Seattle or drive directly to the crag and meet the crew there.

For brand-new climbers: show up, tell them you're new, and you'll be paired with someone who can show you the basics. For experienced climbers: RSVP on the [Gearhouse calendar](https://www.joingearhouse.com/) so they know to expect you and can size the carpool.

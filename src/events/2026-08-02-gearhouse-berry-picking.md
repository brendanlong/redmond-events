A gentler outing for a change of pace: Gearhouse is organizing a morning of **berry
picking at Swans Trail Farms** in Snohomish on Sunday, August 2. The plan is exactly as
relaxed as it sounds — wander the fields, pick fresh berries, poke around the farm, and
come home with a haul for snacking, baking, or jam. It's the kind of low-key, in-the-fresh-air
outing that's easy to say yes to, and a nice one if you want the social-club vibe without
a 3,000-foot climb attached.

It's a straightforward drive north: Swans Trail Farms sits just outside Snohomish, about
a **40-minute drive from downtown Redmond** up past Woodinville — out into farm country
rather than into the city, so it's an easy Sunday. Worth noting the season can shift with
the weather: early August is typically late strawberries rolling into blueberries, but
check the [farm's u-pick page](https://www.swanstrailfarms.com/) for what's actually ripe
that week before you build the day around a specific berry. You pay the farm for what you
pick, so bring a little cash or a card on top of your membership.

RSVP on the [Gearhouse calendar](https://www.joingearhouse.com/) for the meet time and
any carpool. Bring sun hats, water, and containers if you want to haul home more than the
farm's baskets hold — and it's a genuinely family-friendly one.

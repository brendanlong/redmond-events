The Stranger's **Genius Awards** return to the Moore Theatre on Saturday, September 12 — part awards show, part art party. Six local artists get handed $10,000 grants, no strings attached, across Film, Food, Literature, Music, Performance, and Visual Art, celebrating Seattle's cultural scene rather than anyone else's. The night closes with a headlining set from **Shabazz Palaces**, the boundary-pushing Seattle hip-hop duo (Sub Pop, a genuine local institution), followed by a DJ dance party in the basement.

The schedule: VIP pre-party at 5:30 p.m., general doors at 6:30, the awards themselves 7:30-8:30, Shabazz Palaces from 9-10, then dancing until 11. It's less a sit-down ceremony than an all-night party with an awards show embedded in it.

It's a genuine trip into the city — about a **30-minute drive from downtown Redmond** to downtown Seattle, with the usual weekend parking hassle — but this is exactly the kind of one-a-year, can't-get-it-anywhere-else event that clears the higher Seattle bar: a known headlining act, a real sense of local cultural occasion, and a crowd that cares about Seattle's arts scene.

Tickets and details at [strangergeniusawards.com](https://www.strangergeniusawards.com/).

Gearhouse is doing a **weekend coastal campout at Westport, WA** the last weekend of July (Friday the 24th through Sunday the 26th) — and if you've been looking for a reason to get out of the Eastside bubble for a weekend, this is a good one. Westport is Washington's ocean surf town on the Grays Harbor coast, about **2.5–3 hours from Redmond** depending on traffic. It's a real trip, not a day outing, but it's organized by Gearhouse which means carpools, a group camp setup, and the social scaffolding already in place.

The agenda is intentionally loose: surf, beach walking, clamming if you're into it, sitting at camp and doing nothing much with people you like. Gearhouse describes it as a "choose your own adventure weekend" — some people will paddle out for a surf session, others will spend the weekend mastering the art of the campfire. It's the kind of trip where the structure is having a crew and a location, and the rest fills itself in.

From Redmond: take I-90 west to I-5 south, then US-12 west through Olympia and out to the coast. The drive is mostly easy freeway, the kind of thing where a good playlist makes it feel short. This is a carpool event, so coordinate through the [Gearhouse calendar](https://www.joingearhouse.com/) — RSVP there to find out about camping specifics and to lock in a spot.

Westport is far enough that it's a real commitment, but this is exactly the kind of organized outdoor group trip that Gearhouse does well: low planning overhead for you, high payoff for a summer weekend on the Washington coast.

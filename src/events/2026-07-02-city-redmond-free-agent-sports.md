If you've been meaning to get into pickleball but don't have a regular crew to play with, the City of Redmond just solved that problem. **Free Agent Thursday** brings drop-in pickleball to the Old Redmond Schoolhouse Community Center starting **July 2**, with rotating singles and doubles play in a casual, no-team-required format. Show up, get matched, play. That's it.

The Schoolhouse is on NE 80th Street — a straight shot from downtown Redmond, comfortably within walking or biking distance, or a two-minute drive. For a recurring sports program, that's almost embarrassingly convenient.

If pickleball isn't your thing but volleyball is, **Free Agent Friday** starts **July 10** at the same facility — adults-only coed volleyball, same free-agent format. Both programs are run through Redmond Parks & Recreation, so registration goes through the City's Amilia registration system; look up the program on [redmond.gov/ParksRecreation](https://www.redmond.gov/165/Parks-Recreation) or call 425-556-2300 to get the specifics on fees and time slots.

The appeal here is the accessibility: no assembling a team, no league commitment upfront, just showing up and playing with whoever else is there. It's the lowest-friction way to get into either sport this summer without feeling like you're signing up for something serious.

For the two-wheeled crowd, Gearhouse is running a **Summer Bike Ride Series** through August — relaxed, social-paced group rides that roll out from the Gearhouse coffee shop and loop through some of Seattle's best waterfront and neighborhood scenery. No racing pace here: it's built for conversation as much as cardio, with a post-ride beverage stop most weeks.

The route changes each time. **Saturday, July 18** heads toward Seward Park and Lake Washington; **Tuesday, August 4** is a "Post-work Pedal" evening loop north to Green Lake and Gas Works Brewing for pints after; and **Saturday, August 15** offers a choice of two routes — a return trip past Seward Park, or a 24-mile **West Seattle Loop** with about 1,000 feet of climbing for riders wanting more of a workout.

The honest caveat: rides start from the Gearhouse shop on Capitol Hill, so this is a trip into Seattle either way — the Saturday morning rides are the easier sell (light traffic, and you're exploring the city rather than fighting to get into it for work), while the Tuesday evening ride means a weeknight crossing each direction. Worth it if a scenic ride and a beer with the group sounds like a good way to end a Tuesday; skip it if you're not up for the commute on a work night.

RSVP on the [Gearhouse calendar](https://www.joingearhouse.com/) for that week's specific route, meet time, and whether bikes are provided. Bring your own bike and helmet unless the listing says otherwise.

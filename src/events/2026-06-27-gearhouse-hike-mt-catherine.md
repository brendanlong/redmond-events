If you've been meaning to get a real mountain hike on the calendar, **Gearhouse** —
Seattle's outdoor-adventure club — is running a guided group hike up **Mount
Catherine** on Saturday. It's a forested Snoqualmie Pass summit with a payoff view
over Kachess Lake and the surrounding Cascades: enough climbing to feel earned,
short enough to be back by mid-afternoon.

The nice surprise here is the drive. Mount Catherine sits right off the pass, so
from downtown Redmond it's about a **50-minute straight shot east on I-90** — closer
for us than it is for the folks coming from Gearhouse's Capitol Hill home base. Going
with an organized group also takes the friction out of it: you get company, a known
route, and no solo-trailhead logistics.

A note on access: Gearhouse is a membership club (its cafe is open to all, but
organized trips like this generally run for members or by RSVP), so check the
[Gearhouse calendar](https://www.joingearhouse.com/) for sign-up details, the meet
time, carpool plans, and what to bring. Pack the usual: sturdy shoes, layers, water,
and the [Ten Essentials](https://www.nwac.us/) for a Cascades day hike.

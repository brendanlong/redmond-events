Seattle Parks' **Center City Cinema** series screens **The Rocky Horror Picture Show** on the lawn at Cal Anderson Park on Friday, July 31, at **7:30 p.m.** — free, outdoors, and about as far from a quiet movie night as it gets. It's the cult-classic interactive musical-comedy in full: Dr. Frank-N-Furter's castle, the Time Warp, and (depending on the crowd) the usual call-and-response theatrics that have kept this movie a midnight-show staple for 50 years.

Cal Anderson Park is on Capitol Hill — about a **25-30 minute drive from downtown Redmond**, with the usual caveat that Seattle traffic and street parking on a Friday night take some patience. It's a genuine trek, but a free outdoor screening of this particular movie, in this particular park (recently renovated, big open lawn), is the kind of only-in-Seattle novelty that's worth the trip if cult movies and a rowdy communal crowd sound fun. Bring a blanket or low chair and a layer for after dark.

No tickets, just show up and grab a spot on the lawn before it starts filling in. Details at [seattle.gov/parks](https://www.seattle.gov/parks/recreation/events-and-attractions/park-activation-events).

Denver alt-rock band **The Fray** — best known for "How to Save a Life" and "You Found Me" — play Chateau Ste. Michelle's outdoor amphitheater on July 21st. If your Spotify history has any mid-2000s indie rock in it, this is a reliable evening of songs you know every word to, served up at one of the closest major concert venues to downtown Redmond.

The Fray leaned hard into piano-driven, emotionally direct rock when that sound was having its moment, and those songs have aged well enough to hold up in an outdoor winery setting where the vibe skews more relaxed crowd than arena intensity. It's a comfortable summer night format: gates open at 6 p.m., bring your own chairs and blankets, wine from the tasting room is part of the deal.

The venue is in Woodinville, roughly **15 minutes from downtown Redmond** via Redmond–Woodinville Road, with offsite shuttle parking available. Tickets through Ticketmaster; the [full summer lineup](https://www.ste-michelle.com/concerts/summer-concerts) is worth browsing if you want to see what else is on the calendar.

Seattle's biggest summer music festival is back: **Capitol Hill Block Party** takes over 12th Avenue to Broadway for three days, **August 7-9**, with over 100 acts across bars, restaurants, and outdoor stages packed into a few city blocks. This year's headliners are **MUNA** (the queer pop trio bringing their new record *Dancing On The Wall*, and the only Seattle stop on the tour), UK post-punk breakouts **Wet Leg**, and electronic producer **Disco Lines**, with a deep supporting bill that includes Parcels, Magdalena Bay, a Tinashe DJ set, and dozens more — more than half the lineup is Seattle-based.

If your taste runs toward pop with an edge — think Marina or Chappell Roan-adjacent hooks — MUNA is the draw here, and Wet Leg and Magdalena Bay round it out nicely for anyone who likes their pop with sharper guitars or synths. It's less a single-stage concert and more a whole neighborhood turned into a festival: wander between a dozen venues, catch a set, grab food from a sidewalk vendor, repeat.

It's a genuine trip into the city — about a 25-30 minute drive from downtown Redmond on a good day, longer with festival traffic and street closures around Capitol Hill, so plan on transit or a rideshare rather than fighting for parking. But for a lineup this strong, and a festival this singularly Seattle, it clears the bar easily. Three-day GA passes start at $199 plus fees; single-day tickets are also available closer to the date.

Full lineup, set times, and tickets are at [capitolhillblockparty.com](https://www.capitolhillblockparty.com/).

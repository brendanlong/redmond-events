**Japan Fair** takes over the Meydenbauer Center in downtown Bellevue this weekend
for its 10th anniversary — a free, two-day celebration of Japanese arts, culture,
food, and tech. If the Holi celebration at Marymoor was your kind of thing, this is
squarely in that lane: a big, friendly cultural festival you can drop into for an
hour or spend half a day at, no ticket required.

There's a lot packed in. Expect **taiko drumming, traditional dance, and martial
arts** demos on stage; a **cosplay contest**; tea ceremony, ikebana (flower
arranging), and calligraphy demonstrations; rows of exhibitors and shopping; and —
the part worth showing up hungry for — **Japanese food and a sake tasting**. There's
a Kids' Corner with origami and Japanese carnival games, so it works for families
too. It's all indoors and air-conditioned, which is a nice hedge against a hot
June afternoon.

Hours are **Saturday 10 a.m.–6 p.m. and Sunday 10 a.m.–5 p.m.** Bellevue's
Meydenbauer Center is about a **20-minute drive from downtown Redmond** (SR-520 to
I-405), and it's an easy one to combine with anything else you've got going on the
Eastside. Free admission makes it a low-commitment way to spend part of the weekend.

For the food-crawl crowd, Gearhouse is leading an **Urban Food Hike** through Pike Place Market and Belltown on **Sunday, July 26**, a three-hour daytime walk that's equal parts sightseeing and eating. It's a small-plates format: the group stops at three local favorites and shares one appetizer or small dish at each, starting with chowder at **Pike Place Chowder** near the market, then heading into Belltown for a stop at **Pasta Casalinga** before a third stop to close things out.

It's a nice change of pace from Gearhouse's usual hiking-and-paddling lineup — genuinely more "urban hike" than trail hike, but the same idea of moving through a neighborhood with a group and a plan, just with food instead of elevation gain. Good for anyone who likes Pike Place Market but wants an excuse to actually explore past the fish-throwing and into Belltown's side streets.

It's a weekend trip into Seattle rather than a weeknight one, so the traffic calculus is easier — figure a 30-35 minute drive from downtown Redmond, or take the bus if you'd rather not deal with downtown parking on a Sunday.

RSVP on the [Gearhouse calendar](https://www.joingearhouse.com/) to lock in a spot and get the full stop list. Come hungry, but not starving — it's shared small plates, not a full meal at each stop.

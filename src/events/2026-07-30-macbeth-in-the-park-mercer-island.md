**Wooden O** — the touring outdoor Shakespeare company that spends every summer doing free performances in Puget Sound-area parks — brings **Macbeth** to Luther Burbank Park on Mercer Island on Thursday, July 30, at **7 p.m.** It's the full witches-and-prophecy, ambition-and-downfall tragedy, staged outdoors as the sun sets over Lake Washington, running about two hours with no intermission. Fair warning from the production itself: there's stage blood and violence, so it leans more "atmospheric thriller" than kid's-matinee Shakespeare.

Luther Burbank Park sits right on the lake on the north end of Mercer Island — about a **20-minute drive from downtown Redmond** via I-90, easy and traffic-light most weeknights since you're not headed into the city. Bring a picnic dinner, a blanket or low chair, and a layer for once the sun drops.

It's free, no tickets needed — just show up and find a spot on the lawn. (Gearhouse, the Capitol Hill outdoor-social club, is also organizing a group trip to this same show via light rail if you'd rather go with a crowd; check [joingearhouse.com](https://www.joingearhouse.com/) for details.) Full regional schedule and more info at [unionartscenter.org](https://www.unionartscenter.org/macbeth/).

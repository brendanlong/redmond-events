For the hikers chasing a big-payoff day, **Gearhouse** has a guided group climb of
**Crystal Peak** on Saturday, July 11, on the northeast edge of **Mount Rainier National
Park**. It's the kind of hike you train for: about **7 miles round trip with 3,000 feet
of gain**, rated challenging, roughly 5.5 hours of climbing to a summit that stares
straight across at Rainier itself. Wildflower meadows on the way up, an enormous
mountain at the top — this is a standout, not a stroll.

It's the farthest of the summer's hikes: the trailhead is off **SR-410, about a 2-hour
to 2-hour-15 drive south** from the Eastside via Enumclaw. That distance is exactly why
it clears the bar — a guided trip to a Rainier-view summit is worth the early start, and
going with the group means a carpool, a set route, and company for the long miles. Note
that Mount Rainier National Park is on a [timed-entry reservation system](https://www.nps.gov/mora/planyourvisit/reservations.htm)
in summer; the Gearhouse organizers handle the logistics, so check the event notes when
you RSVP.

RSVP on the [Gearhouse calendar](https://www.joingearhouse.com/) for the meet time and
carpool plan. Pack for a strenuous Cascades day: sturdy boots, layers, sun protection,
lots of water and food, and the [Ten Essentials](https://www.nwac.us/). Three thousand
feet of gain earns the view — come ready to work for it.

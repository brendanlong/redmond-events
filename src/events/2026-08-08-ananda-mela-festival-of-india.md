**Ananda Mela** — billed as the largest Festival of India in the Northwest — returns to the Redmond City Hall campus for **August 8-9, noon to 9 p.m. both days**. Expect stage performances of Indian classical and folk dance, a "Vedic Village" built around ancient wellness traditions, an Indian food court, and a full bazaar of shopping and crafts. It's a genuinely big deal locally — past years have drawn around 20,000 people over the weekend.

If you enjoyed the Japan Fair in Bellevue or any of the other cultural celebrations we've covered this summer, this is very much the same category: free, all-ages, and built around cultural performance and food rather than just vendor booths. And unlike most of those, it's **walking distance** — Redmond City Hall is right at the edge of downtown, so this is as easy a yes as a festival gets.

Free admission, food and shopping cost extra. Full schedule at [anandamela.org](https://www.anandamela.org/).

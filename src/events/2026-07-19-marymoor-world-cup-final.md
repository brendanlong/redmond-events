The City of Redmond and King County Parks are hosting a **free community watch party for the 2026 FIFA World Cup Final** at Marymoor Park on **July 19, 10 a.m. to 3 p.m.** — and this one is genuinely right in your backyard. Marymoor is a 10-minute walk or five-minute bike ride from downtown Redmond, which makes this about as easy an event as it gets for a championship soccer match.

The setup: a giant outdoor screen, live entertainment, food vendors, kids' activities, and giveaways. The City is billing it as a community celebration of soccer and culture, and for anyone who's been following the tournament this summer, it's a solid way to watch the final with a crowd rather than alone on a couch.

The 2026 World Cup is the first to be co-hosted by the United States, Mexico, and Canada, which has given the tournament an unusually big local footprint — Seattle and the broader Eastside have seen more soccer buzz this summer than usual, and this is the Redmond community's version of the closing party.

No ticket, no registration needed — just show up. Bring something to sit on; Marymoor's big meadow lawn is ideal for exactly this kind of outdoor viewing event. The match kicks off mid-morning, so you can be home for lunch.

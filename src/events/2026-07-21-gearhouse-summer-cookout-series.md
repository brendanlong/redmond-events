Gearhouse is running a **monthly Summer Cookout Series** from June through September — grills, potluck sides, and a different Seattle park each time, timed for sunset. It's the low-key, come-as-you-are cousin of their bigger holiday cookouts (like the Fourth of July brunch at Madrona Park): show up, eat, hang out by the water or the skyline, go home when you're ready.

**Tuesday, July 21** lands at **Gas Works Park**, the old industrial-plant-turned-park on Lake Union with arguably the best sunset-and-skyline view in the city — a genuinely good pick for a summer evening cookout. The series returns **Tuesday, August 18** at **Judkins Park** in the Central District, a quieter neighborhood green space with its own skatepark and picnic areas.

Both dates are weeknight trips into Seattle, so budget for evening traffic on the way over — roughly 25-30 minutes from downtown Redmond depending on the bridge. That's the honest tradeoff for a grill-out with a view most Eastside parks can't match; if a Tuesday evening works for you, it's a nice reason to make the crossing.

RSVP on the [Gearhouse calendar](https://www.joingearhouse.com/) so they can plan the grill, and bring something to share for the potluck side.

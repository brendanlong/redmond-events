**Pista sa Nayon**, Seattle's Filipino festival, takes over the Seward Park Amphitheater on **Sunday, July 26, from 9 a.m. to 8 p.m.** — a full day of Filipino food, music, dance performances, and community vendor booths on the shore of Lake Washington. If you enjoyed the Holi celebration at Marymoor or Japan Fair in Bellevue, this is squarely the same category: a genuinely fun, culturally rich festival that's worth going to even without a personal connection to the culture, just for the food and the atmosphere.

Seward Park itself is a nice bonus — a forested peninsula park with lake views, so there's an easy walk or picnic to pair with the festival if you want to make a full day of it. It's a straightforward Seattle trip, about a 25-30 minute drive from downtown Redmond via I-90, easier on a Sunday than a weekday.

Full schedule of performances and vendors at [pista.org](https://www.pista.org/). It's free to attend.

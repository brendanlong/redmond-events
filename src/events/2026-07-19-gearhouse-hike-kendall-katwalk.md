If you want one of the Cascades' genuinely iconic days on the calendar, **Gearhouse**
is running a guided group hike to the **Kendall Katwalk** on Sunday, July 19. It's a
section of the Pacific Crest Trail out of Snoqualmie Pass that builds to a narrow ledge
blasted across a granite face — dramatic, photogenic, and a little vertigo-inducing in
the best way. It's a real outing: about **12 miles round trip with 2,600 feet of gain**,
roughly seven hours of hiking, rated intermediate. Dogs are welcome on this one.

The geography works in our favor. The PCT North trailhead sits right at Snoqualmie
Pass, so from downtown Redmond it's about a **50-minute straight shot east on I-90** —
no slog into the city, and closer for us than for the folks driving up from Gearhouse's
Capitol Hill base. Going with the group means a known route and a carpool (this one is
carpool-only, no shuttle), so you skip the solo-trailhead logistics.

The **time listed above is when to meet the group at the trailhead** — the PCT North
lot is about an hour's drive from Gearhouse's Capitol Hill shop, where the carpool
gathers at **8 a.m.**, so plan to be at the trailhead around **9**. Since you'll be
driving straight east from the Eastside rather than into the city, meeting at the
trailhead is the easy move; confirm the exact plan when you RSVP.

Because it's a Sunday and a clean I-90 run, the usual into-Seattle weekday hassle
doesn't apply — just point the car east. RSVP on the
[Gearhouse calendar](https://www.joingearhouse.com/) to confirm the meet time and carpool plan,
and pack for a full Cascades day: sturdy shoes, layers, plenty of water and food, and
the [Ten Essentials](https://www.nwac.us/). Twelve miles is a real effort, so come ready
to move.
